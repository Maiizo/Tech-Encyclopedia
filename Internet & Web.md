## Domain Name System (DNS)

The internet runs on IP addresses (like `192.168.1.1`), but bcuz humans are bad at remembering numbers, The DNS acts like the internet's phonebook, translating human-readable names into machine-readable numbers.

### ccTLDs (Country Code Top-Level Domains)

> [!summary] Definition: ccTLD
> A Country Code Top-Level Domain (ccTLD) is an internet top-level domain generally reserved for a country, sovereign state, or dependent territory. They are always exactly two letters long.

> [!example] Examples:
> * **.tv (Tuvalu)**
> * **.io (British Indian Ocean Territory)**
> * **.ai (Anguilla)**

> [!tip] Fun Fact & Use Case
> The tiny island nation of Tuvalu makes millions of dollars every single year just by licensing out the `.tv` domain. At one point, this single domain contract generated roughly 1/12th of their entire country's gross national income!
